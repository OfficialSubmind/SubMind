How SubMind Scores Work

Research Confidence (0-100%) — How certain SubMind is about its analysis. Based on source quality, data specificity, evidence chains, and how well different sources agree. Above 80% means strong agreement across high-quality sources. Below 50% means limited or conflicting data.

Trust Score (0-100) — How much you should trust this specific report. Measures 16 factors including: Are the sources real and verified? Do they come from authoritative domains (.gov, Reuters, Nature)? Is the evidence specific with real numbers and dates? Do the predictions have logical basis? Higher = more trustworthy analysis.

Source Tiers:
Tier 1 - Primary Documents: Government (.gov), courts, patents, academic papers
Tier 2 - Official Sources: Reuters, AP, BBC, Bloomberg, Nature
Tier 3 - Reputable Analysis: NYT, WaPo, Brookings, universities
Tier 4 - Industry/Trade: TechCrunch, Ars Technica, Wired
Tier 5 - Secondary: Other sources

Hidden Signals — Things SubMind found that most news coverage doesn't highlight. These gaps between the mainstream story and what the raw data shows can be early indicators of shifts the market or public hasn't priced in yet.

Predictions — SubMind's projections based on historical patterns, current signals, and convergence analysis. The percentage is estimated probability, not certainty. Use these as one input for your decisions, not gospel.

Calibrated Confidence — SubMind adjusts its raw confidence score up or down based on 5 factors: trust score quality, quality issues found, contradictions detected, source verification rate, and divergence signal strength. The calibrated number is more reliable than the raw number.

Strategic Intelligence — SubMind generates actionable strategies based on the analysis. The "stance" (Cautious, Urgent, Opportunistic, Neutral) summarizes the overall strategic posture suggested by the data.

Contradictions — When SubMind finds data points that conflict with each other, it flags them. A contradiction doesn't mean the analysis is wrong - it means the situation is genuinely complex and you should be aware of the uncertainty.

Data Integrity Score (0-100) — Measures how internally consistent the analysis is. 100 = no contradictions found. Lower scores mean more conflicting signals were detected.
